RxJS - Rethinking Asynchronous in JavaScript

by Johannes Weber @jowe

Imagine you go for an afterwork drink to the “Pattern Bar” and order a cocktail. The barkeeper recommends you his newly created cocktail. You aggree. He takes a glass, fills it half with “iterator pattern” and half with “observer pattern” and mixes them togehter. You try the drink and get immediately addicted to it. The cocktail is called “RxJS”.

The aim of RxJS is to provide extensions for reactive programming in JavaScript which is also used heavily in Angular 2.
It’s a paradigm which is oriented on data flows to build responsive user experiences that strive to be event-driven, scalable and resilient. Thinking in this way will help you to handle async processes in your app. And that’s a big thing: from bootstraping your app, controlling animations to handling user events and XHRs.
In this workshop you will get to know the power of RxJS, an overview about reactive programming, how it’s used in Angular 2 and how you can simplify your code through the ability to filter, aggregate, compose and project your data streams.
